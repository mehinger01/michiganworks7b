Q2 2025 Executive Director's Report
Michigan Works! Region 7B — July 18, 2025

This quarter continued to reflect the dedication and momentum our team brings to workforce development across our six-county region. Between April and June, we placed 187 individuals into employment — meaningful progress toward our annual targets and a continued demonstration of the value our services provide to job seekers and regional employers alike.

Active enrollment stands at 412 participants, with 94 individuals completing training credentials during the period. Average starting wages for placed participants reached $18.40 per hour, underscoring the quality of the job matches our career advisors are facilitating each day.

On the employer side, we served 63 businesses and received 228 job orders — strong indicators that our business services team continues to build meaningful trust with the regional employer community. Of particular note, we expanded our On-the-Job Training agreements to include four new manufacturing employers in Ogemaw County, and we launched a new CDL training cohort in direct response to regional transportation sector demand.

Across our 21 active grants, total expenditures for the quarter reached $1.24 million, maintaining healthy spend rates and positioning us well heading into Q3.
